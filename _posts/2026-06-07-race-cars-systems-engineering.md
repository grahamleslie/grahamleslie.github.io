I've caught myself before making the comment, "I think I like working on cars as much as I like racing them." I've thought more about this and started to draw a parellel between my hobby and the systems engineering work I do in my day job.

I first tracked the BMW M235i at Harris Hill Raceway in Austin, Texas, learning the car on a lower speed, smaller track with lots of long, sweeping corners. I was fast. I couldn't quite beat the Miatas and S2000s around those corners, but I'd keep base and beat them in the straights.

Then my home tracks became Laguna Seca and Sonoma Raceway. Much higher speed corners, heavier breaking zones, bigger G-forces. The first impediment was oil pressure in the faux-M car in long sweepers. I heard horror stories in the paddock of M235i's like mine grenading as the oil pooled on the far side of the stock pan with sustained G-forces and starved the engine.

A [MMX Baffled Sump Plate](https://www.ecstuning.com/b-mmx-performance-parts/mmr-n55-baffled-sump-plate/mmr20-0501~mmr/) protected from that problem, but created a new one: tires. I couldn't help but destroy the Michelin Pilot Super Sports every six months--no matter how much I dialed in pressures I would wear the shoulders down to the cords. A very expensive problem.

So, I next installed Yokohama Advan Neova AD09 tires and [K-MAC Stage 2 Camber Plates](https://www.ecstuning.com/b-kmac-parts/stage-2-camber-plates-street-track/192616-2n~kma/). With -2 degress of camber and stiffer sidewalls, I could keep my tires from rolling over both while warming up and when heated.

My lap times dropped dramatically until I felt my brakes fade for the first time. I cut my session short and came into the pits to find disclored rings on my rotors and scorched brake pads. So, I wrapped some [Ferodo DS2500 Fronts](https://www.fcpeuro.com/products/bmw-performance-brake-pad-set-ferodo-racing-fcp4611h) and [Rears](https://www.fcpeuro.com/products/bmw-performance-brake-pad-set-ferodo-racing-fcp4663h) around some replacement Zimmerman rotors and can now maintain this level of performance for a full session.

The engineering part of racing is a not a lot different than the engineering part of work. Scale a system, understand its component parts and how they couple, identify bottlenecks, and then iterate on the design to improve performance.
